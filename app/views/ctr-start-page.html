Check a teacher’s record

You must check a teacher’s record before they start working for you in a school or college in England.

If you use a teacher supply agency then you must make sure they’ve checked the teacher’s record.

A teacher’s record tells you whether they:

  • have qualified teacher status or early years teacher status
  • passed their induction or prohibition
  • have national professional qualifications or mandatory qualifications for teaching pupils with sensory impairments

It also tells you if they have been:

  • prevented from teaching by the Teaching Regulation Agency or General Teaching Council for England
  • found guilty of serious misconduct but not prevented from teaching

You can search for a teacher’s record using their last name and date of birth.

You should confirm the teacher’s identity before you check their record.

Qualified teacher learning and skills (QTLS) status

A teacher’s record will not tell you if they have QTLS. You need to check the Society for Education and Training professional register.

School managers and governors

There’s a separate list of people who are not allowed to manage or govern schools. This is called the section 128 list.
